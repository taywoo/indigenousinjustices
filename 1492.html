    1492 Land
    back lane

    Land defenders have been occupying a site in Caledonia, ON that rightfully belongs to them.

    What Happened?

    The 1784 Haldimand Proclamation was a decree that guaranteed Six Nations a tract of land in Ontario as compensation for the alliance during the American Revolution.

    However, much of this promised land was stolen back immediately and was no longer under Indigenous control due to disputes over land rights with the Crown and, later, the Canadian government. Over 230 years later, the Haudenosaunee are fighting to protect their sovereignty over the last remaining parcel of land in Caledonia.

    Private investors are trying to develop condos on their last piece of land. County Mayor Ken Hewitt views 1492 Land Back Lane as an “illegal occupation” and has urged the OPP to take measures against protestors and the barriers set up across the camp. There have been 33 arrests to date.

    “Whatever we do it’s with the next seven generations in mind”

    Skyler Williams

    Williams, a Mohawk, Wolf Clan member of the Six Nations of the Grand River Territory and a 38-year-old father of four, says ensuring future generations have access to the land once held by his ancestors is more important than whatever consequences he may face.

    Monday will mark 100 days since the beginning of the occupation at what Haudenosaunee land defenders have called 1492 Land Back Lane and where Foxgate Developments still hopes to build a 218-unit subdivision, called McKenzie Meadows.
